Before you book

Fair questions.

Do you train on-site or remotely?

Both. Front-line service training works best on-site in Lagos. Standards, SOPs, and lead coaching can be done remotely where it suits your team.

How long does training take?

It depends on team size and goals, but most venues get real change from a focused session or two plus a follow-up. I keep it practical, not a lecture.

Will it disrupt operations?

No. I work around your shifts and can train in small groups so the venue keeps running while your team learns.

Do you train small teams?

Yes. Even a team of three benefits, and small teams often improve fastest because everyone is in the room.

Service standards

Great service is not talent, it is a set of small habits repeated every shift. I turn your standard into teachable moments, arrival to farewell, so every guest gets the same warm, sharp experience.

What you get

  • A clear service standard for each stage of the guest journey
  • The specific behaviours that earn returns and reviews
  • Role-plays so the team practises, not just listens
  • A one-page standard the team can keep
For example: a warm five-second greeting rule and a proper farewell turned a "nice enough" floor into one guests now mention by name.
What it builds a standard on every shift
Included • Upselling that feels like care

Upselling that feels like care

Upselling gets a bad name because most people do it badly. Done right, it lifts the bill and the guest's night at once, because you are guiding them to something they will actually enjoy.

What you get

  • Natural upsell moments mapped to your menu and flow
  • Simple phrases that feel like a recommendation, not a pitch
  • What to suggest, when, and to whom
  • Practice so it becomes second nature
For example: teaching staff to offer the sharing board to arriving groups lifted average spend without a single guest feeling pushed.
Typical result higher spend, happier guests

Included • Complaint handling

Complaint handling

Every venue gets complaints. The winners turn them into saved, loyal guests. I train a calm, consistent way to handle the hard moments so one bad experience does not become a bad review.

What you get

  • A simple, repeatable complaint-handling method
  • What to say and do in the first thirty seconds
  • When and how to make it right without over-giving
  • Practice on the situations you actually face
For example: a calm "listen, own it, fix it" routine turned an angry table into regulars who now ask for the same server.
What it builds saved guests, fewer bad reviews
