Before you book

Fair questions.

How do you decide what to charge?

On three things: what it costs you, what nearby venues charge, and what your guests actually value. I give you pricing with a logic you can explain, not a number pulled from the air.

Will promotions cheapen my brand?

Blanket discounts can. Well-designed packages do the opposite, they give guests a reason to choose you without teaching them to wait for a price cut. That is the whole craft.

How fast do packages start working?

Slow-night and seasonal offers usually show movement within the first few weeks. We track a couple of simple numbers so you see it, not just feel it.

My venue is small. Is this worth it?

Yes. Small venues feel every empty night hardest, and a single good package can change a whole week. Size is not the point, margin is.

Included • Slow-night packages

Slow-night packages

A slow-night package is a designed reason to show up on the nights your venue usually sits empty. Not a price cut, a mini-event with its own name, offer, and hook, so guests feel they are getting something special rather than a discount.

What you get

  • 2 to 3 recurring weeknight formats built for your crowd, for example Ladies' Night, Industry Night, or Live-Band Tuesday
  • A signature offer for each that feels generous but protects your margin
  • A simple run-of-night so staff know exactly what to do
  • The names, hooks, and one-line pitch to promote each one
For example: a lounge turned dead Tuesdays into "Acoustic Tuesdays", a live singer plus a shared small-chops board. Same drinks margin, a full room, and a night guests now plan around.
Typical goal fuller weeknights in 4 to 8 weeks
Included • Seasonal & holiday promos

Seasonal & holiday promos

Nigeria's calendar is full of moments people already want to spend on: Valentine, Easter, Detty December, the ember months, Independence. Planned ahead, each becomes a booked-out window instead of a last-minute scramble.

What you get

  • A 12-month promo calendar mapped to your venue
  • A designed package for each key moment, with offer, capacity, and pricing
  • A simple promotion plan per moment so it actually fills
  • A booking and deposit structure so you are paid before the day
For example: a "Detty December" table plan opened in October with early-bird tables and a VIP tier, so the biggest month of the year is largely sold before it even starts.
Typical goal own the calendar, not chase it
Included • Bundles that lift spend

Bundles that lift spend

Most guests buy one thing and leave money on the table. Bundles and add-ons quietly raise the average bill by making "a bit more" the easy, obvious choice, without anyone feeling upsold.

What you get

  • Combo and add-on menus designed around what already sells
  • Tiered table and bottle packages, good, better, best
  • Simple upsell prompts your team can actually use
  • Pricing that lifts the average ticket while still feeling like value
For example: a "table plus bottle plus small chops" bundle priced just above two separate buys turned single-drink tables into full-spend tables on weekends.
Typical goal higher spend per guest, same footfall
Included • Data-based pricing

Data-based pricing

Most venues price on a guess and never revisit it. I set prices on three things you can defend: what it costs you, what nearby venues charge, and what your guests actually value, then tune it by night.

What you get

  • A cost-and-margin read on your key items
  • A quick competitor price map for your area
  • Night-by-night pricing where demand differs, weekend versus weekday
  • A price list with a clear logic you can explain to anyone
For example: splitting weekend and weekday table pricing lifted weekend revenue without denting the quieter nights, because each night was finally priced for its own demand.
Typical goal more margin, no guesswork
Included • Repeat & loyalty mechanics

Repeat & loyalty mechanics

A first visit is expensive to earn and cheap to repeat, if you give people a reason to come back. Simple loyalty mechanics turn one good night into a habit, so growth compounds instead of resetting every week.

What you get

  • A simple loyalty or stamp mechanic that fits your venue
  • A VIP or regulars tier worth belonging to
  • A birthday and celebrations play, where people spend the most
  • A WhatsApp or list-building step so you can bring guests back on demand
For example: a birthday-table club that gave the celebrant a free bottle filled slow Sundays with paying groups of ten, because one birthday brings the whole circle.
Typical goal more repeat visits, lower ad spend
Included • Launch plan & tracking

Launch plan & tracking

A great package no one hears about is just an idea. The launch plan is the focused rollout that fills the room, plus the few numbers we watch so we know it worked and can tune it.

What you get

  • A step-by-step rollout: what to post and say, what to offer, and when
  • A staff briefing so the team sells it from night one
  • The 3 to 4 numbers to track: covers, average spend, repeat rate
  • A short review to double down on what is working
For example: a two-week countdown before a new weekly night, with a staff script and an early-bird hook, meant the very first edition opened to a near-full room instead of an empty test.
Typical goal a launch that lands, not a quiet test
